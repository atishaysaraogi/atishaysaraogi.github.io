Ride Engineering

Prompt: To design an operational, 1:50 scale flat ride according to the competition prompt. Specifically, to design and implement the controls systems while collaborating with the mechanical team.

Project: Northwestern's team to the Iowa State University Ride Engineering Competition 2022.

Solution: Using an Arduino Uno to with motors, encoders, and drivers to program and implement the ride controls.

Team: Controls team and the larger competition team.

Part selection

I collaborated with the mechanical team to find motors that worked with the required torque and speed to work with the weight of the model and provide 2 G's of acceleration to the mock riders. We ended up using:

  • Arduino Uno
  • Brushed DC motor, motor encoder, and motor driver
  • Brushless motor (for greater torque and speed), brushless motor controller
  • Batteries and power adapter

Designing the ride cycle

With a pause to simulate riders getting on and off, we designed the ride cycle combining elements of existing rides and our own fantasies! I also led the team to program the emergency stop and other functionalities of ride operation, in compliance with the the American Society for Testing and Materials International standards.
