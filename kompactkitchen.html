Kompact Kitchen

Whitespace: Lack of a full set of kitchen utensils for smaller kitchens with effective organization.

Project: Design Thinking and Communication

Solution: The Kompact Kitchen is a concept which uses empty wall space to organize a full set of utensils on a magnetic board.

Team: Justin Lautenberg, Atishay Saraogi, Andrew Stambaugh, Derek Hassan

I learnt to utilize virtual resources!

I learnt about manufacturing methods and setting competitive pricing. But the most importantly, I learnt how to communicate with my team. I was in India due to the COVID-19 pandemic while my team was in the US. I adapted to working remotely at all hours of the day (and especially night), and through good communication and by working hard to collaborate, we delivered a research document we were proud of!

Analyzing the whitespace

  • Surveyed 23 people in target demographic with 74% users validating the need.
  • Benchmarked competitive products to create pricing and utility goals.
  • I worked from India with my team in the US as we conducted the R&D process remotely across 2 continents.

Main components of the Kompact Kitchen

  • A magnetic sheet so that the utensils and knives stick to the boards
  • A stainless steel back
  • A strong Nano Grip adhesive on the back of the stainless steel to mount on the wall
  • Plastic Separators to create space between the magnetic sheet and the stainless steel back

Creating works-like prototypes of utensils

  • Mocked up prototypes and tested to find optimal structure of utensils.
  • Performed feasibility analysis to determine manufacturing methods and pricing.
  • Delivered the product research to client.
