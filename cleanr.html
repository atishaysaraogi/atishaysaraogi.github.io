Cleanr

Problem: Some people with clinical and non-clinical attention deficits struggle to stay organized with their chores and tasks.

Project: Human computer interaction

Solution: Cleanr makes chores manageable and allows users to hold themselves accountable

Team: Alex Chen, Aliyah Tenner, Andy Xiao, Atishay Saraogi, Cece Stumpf, Vikas Jain

I learnt the value of scrappy prototyping!

I learnt about prototyping apps and different ways like roleplaying, Figma, or heatmap analysis to determine the functioning of the app. I also learnt a lot about features that apps commonly use that lack inclusivity towards neurodivergent people and simple practices like changing interface design or fonts that work better for a wider intersection of people.

Defining our target users

  • I conducted 16 interviews over the course of 5 weeks to determine exactly what problem we were solving. I learnt that starting tasks was the most challeneging part for many.
  • User interviews expanded the scope of our problem to all people who struggled with holding attention.
  • We chose to focus on people with ADHD as they had the most specific needs.

Building on the best of existing solutions

  • I analysed the benefits and drawbacks of existing solutions to create benchmarks.
  • Brainstormed ideas and conducted surveys to get feedback from users and other stakeholders like parents.
  • Created a system model, interface model, and user model based on our research.

Figma prototypes for user insight

  • Evolved from paper prototypes to higher fidelity Figma prototypes
  • Analysed risks imposed by prototypes, namely that human presence was an existing solution. Users forget or ignore reminders.
  • I built in customizable reminders that users could define and we created a time recommendation system that learnt from users' habits.
